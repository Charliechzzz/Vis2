Educational level in Western Australia and New South Wales on 2019

Introduction

In recent years, education has been paid more and more attention. The government hopes to raise the level of education to improve the level of science and technology in the country, and parents hope that their children receive a good education to develop a sound personality and professional skills. Therefore, this visualization will start from introducing primary schools, middle schools, and universities in Western Australia and New South Wales, and based on a comprehensive range of indices to evaluate the local educational level.

Quantative evaluations of educational levels

Primary schools and secondary schools in New South Wales and Western Australia

There are some scoring evaluations to present educational level of primary and secondary schools including Bettereducation score and Index of Community Socio-Educational Advantage (ICSEA) score. The following map is to show general educational level of primary schools and secondary schools in Western Australia and New South Wales with Bettereducation score on 2019.

Bettereducation score

Bettereducation score is one of grading algorithms to get the relative educational levels for schhols. The map is using Bettereducation score datasets of every primary schools and secondary schools from Bettereducation. The higher score means better eduacational level and we can see the range of scores is from 70 to 100 from the map legend. The luminance of color is a quantative attribute. We can see that most of primary

and secondary schools area at the coastal city. In addition, the schools are concentrated in southern Western Australia state. By comparision, there are more schools in New South Wales than those in Western Australia. Meanwhile, the number of New South Wales schools gettingg higher Bettereducation score is also larger than that of Western Australia schools.

Based on the above map, the datasets are filtered and calculated some numerical results. One is the median Bettereducation Scores of primary and secondary schools of these two states. For primary schools in New South Wales, the median score is about 83. The median score of secondary schools is 82. However, that score of Western Australia primary schools is up to 85. The highest median

number is 86.5 got by New South Wales secondary school. In addition, the ratios of Bettereducation Score > 90 also are considered to detailed analysis. According to the dot chart, this ratio of New South Wales is still the highest, which proves that New South Wales has a great performance on education not only median aspect but also top aspect.

Index of Community Socio-Educational Advantage score

The Index of Community Socio-educational Advantage (ICSEA) is a scale of socio-educational advantage that is computed for each school which enables visitors to make comparisons between a selected school and all students with a similar background based on the level of educational advantage or disadvantage that students bring to their academic studies. The used dataset is from My School.

It can be seen that the distribution of the top Index of Community Socio-Educational Advantage score is also concentrated at coastal cities in New South Wales and Western Australia. Overall, the Index of Community Socio-Educational Advantage scores map shows New South Wales primary and secondary schools are more competetive than those in Western Australia.

Universities in New South Wales and Western Australia

Universities represents the highest educational level and technical level in states. More top universities means higher levels of higher education.

The New South Wales and Western Australia universities in Top 500 Quacquarelli Symonds (QS) world university rankings are counted. For New South Wales, there are six top universities including the University of Sydney, the University of New South Wales, University of Technology Sydney,

Wollongong Univeristy, Macquarie Univeristy and Western Sydney University. There are two top universities which are the University of Western Australia and Curtin University. The dataset is from Quacquarelli Symonds (QS) world university rankings.

Qualitative evaluations of educational levels

Besides the quantative and numercial evaluations, qualitative evaluations are also necessary when doing comprehensive consideration.

Boys and girls enrollments

The isotype dot plot is showing the relative percentage of boys and girls enrollments of primary school and secondary school in New South Wales and Western Australia. The dataset resoure is Australian Curriculum, Assessment and Reporting Authority. The balance of girls and boys in New South Wales schools is positive on the whole.
